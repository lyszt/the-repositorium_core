# Chapter I — Origins

## First Prototype — January 2014

The first recorded game creation predates the Blade Zenon forum by more than a year. On **January 30, 2014**, the creator reported successfully building a first game. The concept: a **2D adventure** with swords and bows. Sprites were to be drawn in **MS Paint** and cut in Photoshop. No engine name is mentioned — this was a primitive prototype, not a Unity build.

Two months later, on **March 5, 2014**, Unity entered the picture. The creator announced they were downloading **Unity 3D** to build their *"first game"* — framing the January prototype as a stepping stone, not the real start. The stated goal: post on **Steam**, earn money, and eventually visit Japan to meet manga creator Eiichiro Oda.

These two moments — the Paint-sprite prototype and the Unity download — bracket the real beginning of the project.

## The FreeRealms Context

Blade Zenon was born directly out of the shutdown of **FreeRealms**, a Sony Online Entertainment MMORPG that had been a significant game for a generation of young players. When FreeRealms shut down, its community fragmented, searching for something to fill the void.

Ryujinni — then 12 years old — decided to build that replacement himself. Community member Hiruko Kagetane later confirmed this directly, noting the project had originally been intended as a FreeRealms replacement before things slowly fell apart.

## Lygon

Before there was a game, there was a group. **Lygon** was founded in **2013** — not as a studio, but as a friend circle. By 2014 it had become the core creative engine behind multiple game projects, described retrospectively as its *golden era*: a team working together toward the shared ambition of founding a famous games company.

The group's self-description in later years: *"A family of people destined for greatness."*

Lygon's preliminary end came in 2015, with its complete dissolution in 2016.

## Studio Lygon X

The project was developed under the banner of **Studio Lygon X**, credited in the forum's footer as the production entity behind Blade Zenon (2024 archival note).

## Release Date & Platforms

The game was officially targeted for release on **October 24, 2015**, with support announced for:

- **Windows**
- **UNIX Systems**

The forum carried an age rating of **13+**.

## The Forum Launch

The ProBoards forum at `bladezenon.boards.net` served as the project's public face — a place to post news, gather community feedback, coordinate guilds, document lore, and recruit players before the game's release.

Moderators were appointed early: **BigeCZ**, **Darky**, **Lady Nanami**, **Sȶar**, **Aʞasa**, and **Ryujinni** himself.

## The Name Debate

The project's name was never universally agreed upon. Among contributors, an orthographic split persisted between **"Blade Zenon"** and **"Blade Zenom"** — the latter spelling appearing regularly in internal discussions. No official ruling resolved it. The public-facing forum and assets used *Zenon*, but the alternate spelling continued to circulate informally throughout the project's active period.

## Companion Project: Infantaria

Blade Zenon was not the only project under development during this period. A companion project called **Infantaria** (*Infantry*) was in active development as early as January 2014 — over a year before Blade Zenon's forum launched. It was developed by a team of four people and was similarly targeting a **2015 release**, with **Steam** named as the distribution goal.

A surviving beta demonstration shows the game's water graphics engine, labelled *Água Beta* (Water Beta), with an acknowledgement that the visuals were still early-stage.

By July 2015 — just as the Blade Zenon forum was at its busiest — Infantaria was already being recalled with nostalgia by the same contributors who had built it.

A 2018 retrospective assessed Infantaria as a failed project while noting that Blade Zenon had still carried real potential. The same retrospective acknowledged that neither project had ever been finished — ambitious goals that never reached completion.
