# Chapter II — The World

## Premise

Blade Zenon is a dark fantasy set **seven years after** the fall of a hero. A once-celebrated protector was corrupted by a cursed sword, and in the years since, has become the very thing they once fought against. The world now faces destruction at the hands of this **"corrupted protector."**

Standing against the destruction is a single organization: **Numquam Vincar and the Knights of Eden**.

## Player Choice

The game's central design premise gave players agency over their allegiance:

- **Defend the world** — stand with the Knights of Eden against the corrupted protector.
- **Accept its fall** — align with destruction and let the world end.

This moral binary was unusual for a community-built game of this scale and era, and it likely drove much of the early excitement around the project.

---

## The Legend of Zenon

The world's mythology traces back to an ancient human legend: a god named **Zenon** forged a sword of immense power. Humans came to call it the **Blade Zenon**.

The legend is fragmentary by design. Different passages contradict each other; no single version is considered authoritative. This ambiguity was part of the lore's structure — the sword's origins were meant to be debated, not settled.

## The Blade's Curse

The Blade Zenon is not simply powerful. It is dangerous in a specific and insidious way:

Anyone who wields it falls into an unending hallucination — pleasant or surreal dreams that consume the user's mind while their body continues to act in the real world. The wielder experiences nothing but the dream. The world around them experiences the destruction.

No wielder has ever returned to normal. Even celebrated heroes who took up the sword went on to destroy entire cities before being stopped. The sword does not corrupt through ambition or greed — it simply removes the wielder from reality while leaving the damage.

This is why the question of the Blade is never about who is *worthy* to use it. No one is.

## The World of Hyper

The setting is ruled by a government called **Hyper** — a tyrannical power with a medieval ideology operating at a technological scale. Hyper maintains control through thought suppression, public execution of dissenters, and the denial of civic rights. Citizens have no vote and minimal freedom of movement or expression.

The Blade Zenon is one of Hyper's most prized possessions, housed in the government city of **Nanami** and previously in the ancient ruins of **Secilian**, which Hyper fortified heavily with armies, machines, tanks, and sentinel units.

## Factions

### The Knights of Eden / Numquam Vincar

The principal defenders of the world against both Hyper and the corrupted protector. *Numquam Vincar* — "will never be conquered" — is the organization's founding motto.

### Lygon

A rebel faction opposing Hyper. Lygon is the weaker of the two rebel organizations, repeatedly outmaneuvered and defeated in direct confrontation. Rather than pursue military victory, Lygon's strategy shifted toward denying Hyper its most dangerous asset — the sword itself.

The name **Studio Lygon X**, under which Blade Zenon was developed, is a direct reference to this faction.

### Omega

The stronger rebel faction. Omega's leadership is not ideologically opposed to tyranny so much as it is opposed to Hyper specifically. The faction's commander seeks to seize the Blade Zenon and use it to claim the throne — making Omega a rival threat rather than an ally.

## The Zenon Strategy

Lygon's answer to the Blade problem was not to wield it — it was to make it unreachable for everyone.

**Mission objective:** Steal the Blade Zenon from Hyper's custody and sink it to the bottom of the sea.

**Operational plan:**
1. Disguised soldiers deliver barrels of knockout gas into the target facility, incapacitating Hyper personnel.
2. A small team infiltrates through rear access points, bypassing seven armed guards and sniper coverage.
3. The sword is located, secured, and exfiltrated quietly.
4. The sword is transported by air and disposed of at sea.

The mission began breaking down when the transport aircraft was detected by Hyper's SAM network near **Drackmore** and shot down. The plane went down on the island itself — the quarantine zone the mission had been routed to avoid. Over Sapph'Ire Dragon and Ender Vod'uno survived the crash and found themselves stranded on Drackmore.

## The Arise Arc

A separate but connected story thread introduces **Arise** — a girl born in the ruins of **Free Realms**, a world that was destroyed. She leads an organization called **the Union**, which fights against a figure known only as **the Lord**, whose goal is the destruction of neighboring worlds.

Arise and the Union operate under the banner of the Blade Zenon, not as wielders of the sword but as inheritors of its symbolic weight — fighting for peace in the name of the legend. The Union's motto:

> *Might of iron string, Power is what we bring —*
> *United we stand, for the gods we praise,*
> *For more power, so we can arise.*

The in-universe destruction of Free Realms mirrors the real-world shutdown of the FreeRealms MMORPG that prompted the project's creation, making Arise one of the more openly autobiographical elements of the worldbuilding.

## Lore

Formal lore was posted in the **History** board of the forum under the section titled *Official Lore*, which contained 2 threads and 7 posts. The content of those threads remains partially accessible, though most of the detailed worldbuilding appears to have lived in community discussion rather than official documentation.

## Zodiac Pets

One of the more notable community threads was **"Blade Zenon Zodiac Pets"**, posted by moderator **Lady Nanami** in July 2015. The thread accumulated **23 replies** and **1,681 views** — the highest view count of any recoverable thread — suggesting creature/pet design was a major point of enthusiasm among early members.

## Voice Work

Ryujinni posted a thread titled **"Lord Voice Test!"** in the News board (August 2, 2015), a poll with 5 replies and 689 views. This indicates active work on voice acting or audio design for the game's characters.

## The Main Menu: 2015 vs 2016

The clearest surviving record of the game's visual development is its main menu, captured a year apart. The build evolved substantially between 2015 and 2016 — including a working title change to **"Zero"**.
